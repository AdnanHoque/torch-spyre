METRICS
  kernel_ms  PRIMARY. Sum of the op's compute kernel bundle(s), excluding
             Memset/Memcpy. This is the work-division-relevant metric (it is
             what the cost model's split choice actually moves) and matches the
             "matmul kernel_ms" convention of the baseline report. Lead with it.
  spyre_ms   SECONDARY. Total device time including fixed per-op overhead
             (stickify, HBM copies). That overhead is roughly constant per op,
             so on tiny isolated kernels (e.g. single-token decode) it dominates
             and is NOT representative of in-model behaviour where it amortises.
             Where kernel_ms and spyre_ms disagree, kernel_ms is the truth for
             work-division; spyre_ms is shown for completeness.
  ratio = tsp / sendnn ; < 1.0 means tsp is faster.

READING THE NUMBERS
  Matmul wins (kernel_ms, work-division):
    - QO bs1 0.87, QO bs4 0.96, MLP bs4 0.85, mlp(swiglu) prefill 0.83 -- tsp faster.
    - Single-token decode K-split is the standout: KV-decode 0.93, Qwen KV-decode
      0.78. These are the rows where split-K fills the otherwise-idle array (the
      narrow-N decode home); tsp BEATS the backend. QO-decode 1.00 is the control
      (N alone fills 32 cores, no K-split, parity).
    - MLP bs1 1.18: improves the suite's worst shape from the heuristic's 1.51 to
      1.18 by enabling the planner. Still > 1.0 (kernel-level gap below ~1.2, not
      work-division); and (8,4) is ~10% off the empirical optimum (4,8) -- a
      documented split-choice miss that is not landable (the adjacent priority
      shape N=12288 wants (8,4) by +57%).
    - KV bs1 1.06: kernel-level. The planner already picks the best measured split.
  Decomposed ops (softmax/layernorm/attention/decode mlp): the work-division
  planner has no lever; the gap is kernel fusion (sendnn fuses; tsp decomposes).
  See the +/* footnotes.

NOTE on sendnn_split: torch_sendnn compiles through deeptools but exposes no
  work-division (numWkSlices) from the Python/torch side. The deeptools SDSC/
  perfdsc dump knobs (PERFDSC_DUMP_DIR, DEEPRT_EXPORT_DIR, DumpSdsc) produce no
  artifact through sendnn's compile path; no on-disk cache or stdout work lines
  are emitted. Marked n/a honestly (not fabricated). tsp_split is the planner's
  chosen (m,n,k) from numWkSlicesPerDim_ (labels mb/out/in -> m/n/k); for
  multi-kernel ops it is the dominant kernel's split.
