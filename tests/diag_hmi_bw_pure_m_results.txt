## Effective HMI BW (subtracted method)

  Broadcast-model effective BW: median=50.9 GB/s, min=39.9, max=143.1
  Replicated-model effective BW: median=1515.6 GB/s, min=1260.9, max=3402.7

## Re-derived BW assuming wall = LF + bytes/BW (HMI fully serial w/ launch floor)

For HMI-dominated rows where wall ≫ LF and wall > compute, the cleanest
read is hmi_ms = wall - LF (compute overlaps HMI; no second subtraction):

| shape | wall - LF (ms) | bytes (MB) | implied BW (GB/s) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| B=128MB M=64  | 3.30 | 136 | 41 |
| B=128MB M=512 | 3.38 | 151 | 45 |
| B=235MB M=64  | 5.93 | 238 | 40 |
| B=235MB M=512 | 5.59 | 259 | 46 |
| B=256MB M=64  | 6.82 | 272 | 40 |
| B=256MB M=512 | 6.38 | 294 | 46 |

M=64 rows give 40 GB/s consistently.
M=512 rows give 45-46 GB/s — slightly higher because compute (2-4 ms)
overlaps part of HMI, leaving smaller hmi_ms in the denominator.

## Verdicts

  - Broadcast model is correct: replicated-model BW is ~1500 GB/s
    (absurd), so B is NOT 32x replicated under pure-M.
  - Effective BW under broadcast accounting: **~40 GB/s**, not 67.
    The 67 GB/s spec figure is the chip's HMI port nameplate; under
    matmul-template ring-broadcast the achieved BW asymptotes lower.
  - Wall formula: **wall ≈ LF + max(compute, hmi)** for HMI-bound,
    where LF (3 ms) and HMI run serially but compute overlaps both.
  - The two B=512MB rows trip per-core EAR overflow (256 MB hardware
    limit) — needs k-split or n-split to fit, so pure-M can't probe
    that regime directly.
