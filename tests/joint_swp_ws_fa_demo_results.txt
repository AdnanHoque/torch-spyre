# FA-2 joint SWP+WS demonstration

# Real per-op walls measured on AIU (Llama 70B-style attention)
# Shape: (1, 8, 1024, 128), k_tile = 128 → 8 K-tiles

## Regime A: unfused (today's torch_spyre decomposition path)

  9 ops per tile, each its own AIU kernel launch with LF=3ms.
  Per-op walls measured directly.

| schedule | wall ms | speedup vs serial |
|---|---:|---:|
| serial | 193.96 | 1.00× |
| decoupled | 172.75 | 1.12× |
| joint | 172.75 | 1.12× |

## Regime B: fused (custom SDSC kernel — what we'd build)

  4 ops per tile (PT QK, fused softmax, PT PV, fused update).
  Each fused stage = 1 LF + work, vs unfused 5+ LFs.

| schedule | wall ms | speedup vs serial |
|---|---:|---:|
| serial | 119.20 | 1.00× |
| decoupled | 96.94 | 1.23× |
| joint | 76.18 | 1.56× |

## Cross-regime comparison

| comparison | wall ms |
|---|---:|
| Unfused serial (today's AIU)         | 193.96 |
| Unfused joint (best ILP can do today)| 172.75 |
| Fused serial (custom kernel only)     | 119.20 |
| **Fused joint (custom kernel + joint SWP+WS)** | **76.18** |

  Reference (AIU's bmm+softmax+bmm path): 17.80 ms (measured)

## Headline

  Today (unfused, serial):              194.0 ms
  Joint scheduling alone (no fusion):    172.8 ms  (1.12× speedup)
  Fusion alone (no joint scheduling):    119.2 ms  (1.63× speedup)
  **Fusion + joint scheduling combined**: 76.2 ms  (2.55× speedup)

## Reading

  - Joint scheduling alone gives modest gains because per-op LF
    dominates: each tensor op's 3 ms LF can't be amortized by
    scheduling, only by fusion.
  - Fusion alone gives big gains by reducing op count.
  - The full win requires BOTH: fusion to reduce LFs, AND joint
    scheduling to overlap PT and SFP across iterations.
  - This is the FA-3 ping-pong pattern, demonstrated on a real
    AIU workload with measured numbers, not synthetic estimates.
